<h3>Common Parts and their Functions</h3>
<p>The Nissan K21 engine consists of several key parts that contribute to its operation.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Valve Cover</strong>: This part protects the engine's internal components and maintains oil circulation. Regular checks help prevent oil leaks.</li>
<li><strong>Oil Pan</strong>: The oil pan stores the engine oil. Keeping it clean ensures proper lubrication and cooling of the engine.</li>
<li><strong>Pistons and Rings</strong>: These parts are crucial for compression and fuel combustion. Worn pistons or rings may lead to reduced power and efficiency.</li>
<li><strong>Fuel Injectors</strong>: They deliver fuel into the engine for combustion. Clogged injectors can cause poor fuel efficiency and engine performance.</li>
<li><strong>Timing Belt</strong>: This belt ensures the engine's components work in sync. A broken belt can lead to severe engine damage.</li>
</ul>
<p>Understanding these components allows for targeted maintenance and promotes a longer engine life.</p>
<h3>Maintenance Schedule and Tips</h3>
<p>Regular maintenance of the Nissan K21 engine is essential to keep it running smoothly. </p>
<p>A typical maintenance schedule includes:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Oil Changes</strong>: Change oil every 250 hours of operation or as specified in the manual.</li>
<li><strong>Filter Replacement</strong>: Replace the oil and air filters at each oil change to avoid contaminants.</li>
<li><strong>Fluid Checks</strong>: Regularly check coolant, hydraulic fluid, and brake fluid levels.</li>
<li><strong>Inspect Belts</strong>: Check for wear or damage on the timing belt every 500 hours.</li>
<li><strong>Clean the Engine</strong>: Keep the engine and its surrounding areas free from debris to prevent overheating.</li>
</ul>
<p>By following these maintenance tips, operators can enhance engine performance and minimize unexpected breakdowns. </p>
<p>Regular attention to these areas will lead to a more reliable forklift experience.</p>
