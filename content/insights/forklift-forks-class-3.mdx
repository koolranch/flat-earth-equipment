Forklift forks, essential for the lifting and handling of materials, come in various classes, with Class 3 being a common category. The specifications of these forks play a crucial role in their strength and suitability for different load types.
<h3>Class 3 Fork Design</h3>
Class 3 forks are designed for heavier lifting compared to Class 2, with a greater load center typically at 24 inches.

The design includes a specific <strong>mounting class</strong>, which in this case is the ITA hook type, ensuring a secure fit to the forklift carriage.

These forks are widely used in industries where larger and heavier materials require transportation.
<h3>Dimensional Attributes</h3>
<strong>Fork dimensions</strong> for Class 3 typically feature <strong><a href="https://flatearthequipment.com/attachments/loader-bucket-pallet-forks/">fork lengths</a></strong> ranging from 42 to 96 inches and <strong>fork widths</strong> that can vary according to the required capacity.

<h3>Appropriate Fork Usage</h3>
Class 3 forklift forks are designed for specific load capacities and heights.

Operators must select the correct <a href="https://flatearthequipment.com/parts/attachments/forks/">fork size</a>, typically ranging from <strong>36”</strong> to <strong>96”</strong> in length, to handle their material load safely.

Using an undersized fork for a heavy load can lead to safety hazards.

Fork orders need to specify the right <strong>capacity</strong> and <strong>height</strong> for the task.

<strong>ITA carriages</strong> should match the forklift blades by size and class to ensure a secure connection.

Forks should be correctly inserted into the carriage with the <strong>positioning lock</strong> securely in place, strictly following the <strong>ISO 2330</strong> standards.
