<h3>Daily and Periodic Maintenance</h3>
<p>Daily maintenance involves regular checks and servicing to keep the machine in good working condition. </p>
<p><strong><a href="https://flatearthequipment.com/uncategorized/importance-forklift-chains-maintenance-safety/">Daily checks</a></strong> include inspecting the battery isolator, verifying fluid levels, and ensuring the <strong>fire extinguisher</strong> is accessible.</p>
<p><em>Check the battery location</em> under the engine bay bonnet. It is crucial to monitor the battery's charge and look for signs of wear.</p>
<p><strong>Periodic maintenance</strong> should be done according to the service manual. </p>
<p>This includes checking the <strong>transmission</strong>, <strong>hydraulics</strong>, <strong>steering column</strong>, and <strong>axle</strong>. </p>
<p>Our <strong>dry lubricant system</strong> minimizes wear on moving parts like wear pads, reducing the need for frequent lubrication. </p>
<p>Follow service intervals to inspect HVAC systems and clean air filters.</p>
<h3>Common Issues and Solutions</h3>
<p>Common issues with JCB telehandlers include hydraulics failure, battery drainage, and HVAC malfunctions. </p>
<p>Hydraulic issues can often be traced back to fluid levels or leaks in the system. Regular checks and timely replacements prevent these problems.</p>
<p>Battery drainage is another frequent problem. </p>
<p>Always ensure the battery is adequately charged and terminals are clean. If the battery continues to drain, it may need replacement.</p>
<p>HVAC systems can become less effective due to clogged filters. </p>
<p>Cleaning or replacing filters can maintain proper airflow. The service manual provides detailed instructions on troubleshooting these systems.</p>
<p>Consult your JCB dealer for complex issues that require professional service.</p>
