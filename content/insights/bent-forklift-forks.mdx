<a href="https://flatearthequipment.com/parts/attachments/forks/">Forklift forks</a> are an essential component of material handling operations, designed to carry and lift heavy loads.

However, these forks are subject to wear and tear and can sometimes bend. Bent forks can arise from a variety of situations including collisions, improper loading, and overloading.

<a href="https://flatearthequipment.com/uncategorized/selecting-forklift-forks-guide/">Identifying bent forklift forks</a> is crucial to maintaining workplace safety and operational integrity. The forks must remain straight to ensure the safe handling of materials.

If a bend is present, it could lead to uneven lifting, which in turn may result in load slippage or tipping, potentially causing damage to products or injury to workers.

When forklift forks do bend, it's important to understand the correct course of action.

Attempting to <a href="https://www.forkliftinventory.com/blog/straighten-bent-forklift-fork/">straighten a bent forklift fork</a> is generally not advisable due to the risk of compromising the metal's integrity.

Instead, industry standards typically require the replacement of damaged forks to ensure the highest level of safety and compliance with regulations.
