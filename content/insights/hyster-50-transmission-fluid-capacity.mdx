<h2>How to Check and Fill Fluid Without Screwing It Up</h2>
<p>This is how you do it in the field, not some climate-controlled dealer bay. Get this wrong and you&#39;ll either cook the transmission or blow the seals. No second chances.</p>
<p>The biggest mistake rookies make is checking the fluid cold. You <strong>have</strong> to check it at operating temp. A cold check is a lie and will make you overfill it every time. Run the machine for <strong>15 minutes</strong>, cycle the hydraulics, and shift it through forward and reverse to get the fluid hot and moving.</p>
<h3>The No-BS Procedure</h3>
<ol>
<li><strong>Level Ground, Neutral:</strong> Park it on level ground. If you can&#39;t find any, make some. Parking brake on, transmission in <strong>neutral</strong>.</li>
<li><strong>Clean a Spot:</strong> Wipe the filth off the area around the dipstick tube. Don&#39;t be the guy who dumps dirt straight into a clean transmission. Pull the stick.</li>
<li><strong>Fill Slow:</strong> If it&#39;s low, add fluid slowly. This isn&#39;t a race. Use a funnel. Add a little, check a little. Don&#39;t overfill it like an idiot.</li>
</ol>
