<h3>Basics of Fault Codes and ECU</h3>
<p>Fault codes, or error codes, are alphanumeric strings displayed by the telehandler's Electronic Control Unit (ECU). </p>
<p>The ECU monitors various systems and components, identifying abnormalities. </p>
<p>When an issue arises, the ECU generates a fault code, signaling the specific problem area.</p>
<p>The ECU plays a central role in machine diagnostics. </p>
<p>By storing these codes, it allows operators and technicians to identify and address issues quickly. </p>
<p>Accessing these codes typically requires a diagnostic tool or an onboard display system.</p>
<p>Regular monitoring and interpretation of these fault codes can prevent minor issues from escalating into major repairs.</p>
<h3>Common JCB Fault Codes and Descriptions</h3>
<p>JCB telehandlers use specific codes to indicate different faults. </p>
<p>For instance, one common error is <strong>P1104-00</strong>, which often signals a general sensor fault. </p>
<p>Similarly, <strong>P1106-00</strong> might indicate an intake air temperature sensor error.</p>
<p>Another frequent fault is <strong>Boom Not Stowed</strong>. This code signifies the boom is not properly positioned when the machine is shut down, posing a safety risk. </p>
<p>Another issue could be <strong>Hydraulic Oil Overheat</strong>, where the hydraulic oil temperature is excessively high due to an overloaded system or low oil levels.</p>
<p>By cataloging these error strings, operators can maintain their equipment more effectively and ensure continuity in their work processes.</p>
