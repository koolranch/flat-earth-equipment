In the high-stakes world of construction and earthmoving, the difference between a project finishing on time and a week of idle machinery often boils down to a single identification number. For owners of **Case loaders**, your serial number (PIN - Product Identification Number) is the DNA of your machine.

## üîç Where Is It? Model-Specific Plate Locations

Case has utilized several primary locations for their identification plates over the years. Use the guide below based on your machine type.

### 1. Backhoe Loaders (580, 590, 695 Series)
For the modern N-Series and older M-Series backhoes, look in these three spots:
- **Primary Plate:** Located on the **left-hand side of the front frame**, near the steering cylinder or pivot area.
- **Cab Riser:** On many N-Series units, a secondary plate is affixed to the **exterior side of the cab**, near the operator's entry step.
- **ROPS Pillar:** Look for a stamped tag on the rear ROPS (Roll-Over Protective Structure) post.

### 2. Wheel Loaders (521, 621, 721 through 1121 Series)
- **Articulated Joint:** The primary PIN plate is typically found on the **right-hand side of the front frame**, just ahead of the articulated steering joint.
- **Chassis Stamp:** For permanent identification, Case often stamps the serial number directly into the **frame rail near the front right wheel**.

### 3. Skid Steers & Compact Track Loaders (SR, SV, TR, TV Series)
- **Main Frame:** Look on the **right-hand side frame rail**, often protected by the loader arm when in the down position.
- **Seat Deck:** On some B-Series models, the plate is tucked **under the operator's seat** or on the firewall inside the cab.

## üìã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### **Q: Is the PIN the same as the Serial Number?**
In the Case world, "PIN" stands for Product Identification Number and refers to the entire machine. "Serial Number" is often used for individual components like the engine or the bucket.

### **Q: Why does my Case serial number start with "JAF"?**
The "JAF" prefix is common for Case machines built at North American facilities. It is part of the CNH Industrial global tracking system.

### **Q: Can I look up my Case build date for free?**
Yes! Use our tool above or the official **[Case MyCNH](https://www.casece.com/)** portal. Official portals often require an account for detailed build sheets.

### **Q: What if my plate is painted over?**
Use a light solvent or very fine sandpaper on the frame rail near the front right wheel to reveal the stamped chassis number.
