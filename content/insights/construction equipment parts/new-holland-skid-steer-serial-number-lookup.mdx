## üîç Where Is It? Model-Specific Plate Locations

New Holland has shifted plate locations over the decades. Use the following guide to find your serial number based on the series of your machine.

### 1. The 300 Series & 200 Series (Modern Models)
For the L200, L300, C200, and C300 series, the identification plate is typically found on the **right-hand side of the machine frame**, near the operator‚Äôs compartment. 
- **Pro Tip:** Look just behind the front wheel (or track) on the frame rail. In modern machines, this is a durable metal plate often protected by a slight indentation in the frame to prevent scratching from debris.

### 2. The LS and L Series (Legacy Models)
Older models like the LS170, LS180, L553, and L175 have more varied locations:
- **Main Frame:** Often located on the **inner wall of the frame** near the operator's left or right foot.
- **Seat Deck:** Some early LS models have the plate tucked **under the operator's seat** or on the riser below the seat.
- **Rear Pillar:** Look on the ROPS (Roll-Over Protective Structure) pillar on the rear left side.

### 3. Alternative Identification (When the Plate is Missing)
If your plate has been painted over or damaged, you can often find the serial number:
- **Stamped into the Frame:** Look for a 7- to 12-digit number stamped directly into the steel on the right-side frame rail.
- **Engine Block Plate:** While this is the *engine* serial number, New Holland dealers can often cross-reference it back to the machine PIN.
- **ECU Diagnostics:** On 2020+ models, the serial number can be retrieved digitally via the onboard display in the "Service" or "About" menu.

## üìã Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### **Q: Is the VIN the same as the Serial Number?**
In construction equipment, they are often used interchangeably. However, "PIN" (Product Identification Number) is the technically correct term for the entire machine, while "Serial Number" often refers specifically to the engine or hydraulic components.

### **Q: Can I look up my serial number online for free?**
Yes! You can use the **[MyCNHi Portal](https://my.newholland.com/)** or our own lookup tool. While official portals require registration, our tool provides quick estimations based on public records.

### **Q: Why does my New Holland serial number start with "N"?**
The "N" prefix usually indicates a North American production unit, most commonly from the Grand Island, Nebraska plant.

### **Q: What if my serial number is only 6 digits?**
Older New Holland (and Ford-New Holland) machines often used shorter numeric sequences. These require legacy parts catalogs. Contact our specialists if you have a 4- to 6-digit number.
