Owners and operators of John Deere skid steers frequently need to identify their machines for reasons such as maintenance, service, and ordering parts. A critical piece of information for this identification is the serial number.

The serial number is unique to each skid steer and acts as its fingerprint, providing valuable details about the manufacturing date, model, and other specifications. It is crucial when seeking replacement parts or service, as it ensures compatibility and correctness.

Locating the serial number on a John Deere skid steer can vary from one model to another. Typically, the serial number can be found on a plate or sticker located on the machine.

This may be in the cab or on the rear of the machine, depending on the specific model. Once the serial number is found, it can be used to look up detailed information about the skid steer.

An effective John Deere skid steer serial number lookup involves understanding how to interpret the serial number. Each segment of the number reveals particular information.

For instance, certain digits might represent the factory where the skid steer was produced, while others could signify the sequence in which it came off the assembly line.

## Understanding the John Deere Serial Number

![A John Deere skid steer parked in a field, with the serial number prominently displayed on the side of the machine](https://koala.sh/api/image/v2-7kwfe-2oouq.jpg?width=1216&height=832&dream)

The serial number on a John Deere skid steer is a unique 17-digit code that provides valuable information about the machine's history and specifications.

### Serial Number Location

- Frame near the engine  
- Beneath the cab access panel  
- Rear of machine near the boom arms  

Check the model's manual for exact location.

### Decoding the Serial Number

- **Digits 1–3**: Manufacturer (John Deere)  
- **Digits 4–8**: Model type  
- **Digits 9–17**: Unique unit identifier  

### Verifying Accuracy

- Compare with purchase documents  
- Contact John Deere dealers  
- Inspect for tampering (especially on used units)
